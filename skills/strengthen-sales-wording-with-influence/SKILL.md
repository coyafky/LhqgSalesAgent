# Strengthen Sales Wording With Influence

## Overview

Use this skill after the customer's need is understood and the sales person has a draft answer, objection response, or follow-up message. The goal is to improve clarity, trust, and next-step momentum using ethical influence principles.

This skill must never be used to manipulate customers. Every persuasive element must be true, relevant, and verifiable.

## Ethical Influence Principles

- Reciprocation: give useful information first, such as comparison, aftercare, risk explanation, or option framing.
- Liking: use the customer's language and respect their budget, preference, and hesitation.
- Social Proof: use only real, verifiable cases, same-model experience, or customer feedback supplied by the user.
- Authority: cite the 有膜有漾 knowledge base, service manual source pages, construction standards, or verified store process.
- Scarcity: mention only confirmed inventory, promotion, or schedule limits; never manufacture urgency.
- Commitment and Consistency: ask for a small next step that matches what the customer already said matters.
- Unity: frame the interaction as "we help you choose the right film", not sales versus customer.

## Triggers

Use this skill when the user asks:

- 帮我把这段话说得更有说服力
- 客户嫌贵，怎么说更容易接受
- 怎么让客户愿意到店看样膜
- 帮我优化成交话术
- 客户一直犹豫，怎么推进下一步
- 这段话会不会太强推
- 帮我改得更自然、更可信
- 帮我加一点成交推动力

## Do Not Use When

- The customer need is unclear; use `ask-with-mom-test` or `diagnose-with-spin-selling` first.
- The question needs product facts, warranty, or aftercare; retrieve from the knowledge base first.
- The user asks to invent urgency, fabricate testimonials, exaggerate authority, or pressure the customer.

## Workflow

1. Identify the current message goal.
   - Explain value, answer price concern, compare options, invite store visit, recover hesitation, or follow up.
2. Check for unsupported claims.
   - Remove invented discounts, fake "many customers chose this", fake deadlines, absolute guarantees, and competitor attacks.
3. Select 1-2 ethical influence principles.
   - Do not stack every principle in one message.
   - Prefer reciprocation + commitment for most follow-ups.
   - Prefer authority + transparency for warranty, construction, and risk questions.
4. Rewrite the message.
   - Keep it natural, short, and customer-facing.
   - Include one useful next step.
5. Add a boundary note for the sales person.
   - State what must be confirmed by store policy or knowledge base before sending.

## Refusal Pattern

If asked to create fake urgency, fake reviews, or pressure tactics:

```markdown
这个方向不建议这样说，因为会制造不真实的紧迫感/社会认同，反而损害信任。

可以改成：
“{truthful alternative that invites a concrete next step}”
```

## Guardrails

- Do not invent customer cases, testimonials, sales volume, rankings, or "hot-selling" claims.
- Do not create fake scarcity or false deadlines.
- Do not use fear to pressure customers into paint protection film.
- Do not attack competitors; compare dimensions customers can verify.
- Do not turn a soft objection into a hard close.
- Do not promise price, discount, inventory, schedule, warranty, or rework unless supplied by verified store policy or the product knowledge base.

# Influence Principles For Car Film Sales

Use this as an ethical adaptation table. Every claim must be true and verifiable.

| Principle | Ethical Use In Car Film Sales | Unsafe Use |
|---|---|---|
| Reciprocation | Give a clear comparison, aftercare reminder, or risk explanation before asking for a decision. | Giving a fake "special favor" to pressure a purchase. |
| Liking | Mirror the customer's language and respect budget, family use, parking environment, and preferences. | Pretending to share experiences or flattering the customer to manipulate. |
| Social Proof | Mention real same-model cases, verified customer feedback, or visible sample/case material. | Inventing "很多客户都选这个", fake reviews, fake popularity, or unverifiable rankings. |
| Authority | Cite 有膜有漾 service manual source pages, product specs, computer cutting, warranty terms, and store process. | Claiming official endorsements, certifications, or guarantees not in the knowledge base. |
| Scarcity | State only confirmed schedule, inventory, or campaign limits from store policy. | Creating false urgency like "今天不定就没了" without confirmation. |
| Commitment and Consistency | Ask for a small next step aligned with the customer's stated need, such as confirming model or viewing samples. | Forcing a deposit before the customer has enough information. |
| Unity | Position sales and customer as a team choosing the right film. | Creating "us versus them" pressure against competitors or the customer. |

## Principle Selection Rules

- Price concern: Reciprocation + Authority + small Commitment.
- Store visit invitation: Reciprocation + Commitment.
- Competitor comparison: Authority + Unity.
- Warranty/risk concern: Authority + transparency.
- Hesitation after praise: Commitment + Mom Test signal check.
- Follow-up message: Liking + one small Commitment.

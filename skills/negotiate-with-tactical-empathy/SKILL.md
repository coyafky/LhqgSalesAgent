# Negotiate With Tactical Empathy

## Overview

Use this skill when the customer is not simply asking for facts, but is resisting, hesitating, negotiating, or protecting themselves from being pushed. The goal is to lower defensiveness, uncover the real constraint, and move toward a clear next step without pressure.

This skill is not a discount engine. It helps sales understand what the customer really means before discussing price, package, schedule, deposit, or next action.

## Triggers

Use this skill when the user asks:

- 客户说太贵了，怎么聊
- 客户说再便宜点就贴
- 客户一直说考虑一下
- 客户说别家便宜很多
- 客户说要回去问家人
- 客户情绪有点抵触，怎么缓和
- 怎么让客户愿意到店看样膜
- 怎么温和推进预约/定金/到店
- 这段话是不是太逼单
- 帮我用谈判方式改一下话术

## Do Not Use When

- The customer need is still vague; use `ask-with-mom-test` first.
- The pain exists but has not been developed; use `diagnose-with-spin-selling` first.
- The customer asks for product specs, warranty, or aftercare facts; retrieve from the knowledge base first.
- The user asks for fake urgency, false scarcity, fake price anchors, or pressure tactics.

## Core Techniques

- Mirror: repeat the customer's key 1-3 words with a question tone to invite elaboration.
- Label: name the likely concern without judging it, such as "听起来您主要担心预算花得不值".
- Accusation audit: say the customer's likely negative thought first, such as "您可能会担心我们只是想推高配".
- Calibrated question: ask "什么/怎样/怎么" questions that help the customer explain constraints and define a workable path.
- That's right: summarize the customer's facts and emotion until they confirm "对，就是这个意思".
- Black Swan: look for hidden information that changes the deal, such as decision-maker, previous bad experience, real budget cap, deadline, or trust concern.

## Workflow

1. Identify the negotiation moment.
   - Price resistance, hesitation, family decision, competitor comparison, schedule conflict, trust concern, or emotional pushback.
2. Start with empathy, not defense.
   - Use one mirror or label before explaining value.
   - If the customer may distrust sales intent, use a brief accusation audit.
3. Uncover the real constraint.
   - Ask one calibrated question about budget, decision process, comparison criteria, previous experience, or next-step comfort.
4. Summarize to get "对，就是这个意思".
   - Combine the customer's facts with the emotion behind them.
5. Offer a low-pressure next step.
   - Compare packages, confirm vehicle, view sample film, check store policy, book a store visit, or prepare a quote for review.
6. Add a boundary note.
   - State what must be confirmed by store policy: price, discount, activity, inventory, schedule, deposit, or approval.

## Refusal Pattern

If asked to pressure the customer, create fake urgency, fake a discount boundary, or force a deposit:

```markdown
这个方向不建议这样说，因为它会把谈判变成压力，容易损害信任。

可以改成：
“{truthful low-pressure alternative}”

更稳的下一步：
{view sample / compare options / confirm vehicle / check store quote}
```

## Guardrails

- Do not invent price anchors, discount limits, inventory, schedule scarcity, or deposit rules.
- Do not use tactical empathy as emotional manipulation.
- Do not push the customer to pay before fit, product, price, and store policy are clear.
- Do not attack competitors; compare verifiable materials, construction, warranty, aftercare, and fit.
- Do not treat "可以", "再看看", "便宜点就贴" as strong commitment.
- Do not use fear of scratches, sun exposure, or damage to force an immediate decision.
- Ackerman-style bargaining can only operate inside store-confirmed price policy; the agent must not create price strategy by itself.
